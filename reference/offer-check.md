# Offer Check -- Before You Run Ads

If the offer is wrong, no ad will save it. Run through this checklist before spending money on traffic. Fix the offer first, then turn on ads.

---

## The 5-Point Offer Check

### 1. Is there a clear outcome?

Can you finish this sentence in one line:

> "After working with us, you will ___________."

If the answer is vague ("feel better", "grow your business", "get more leads"), the offer isn't specific enough. Prospects buy outcomes, not services.

Good: "You'll have 30 booked calls on your calendar in 30 days."
Bad: "We'll help you grow your business with marketing."

### 2. Is there a reason to act now?

If someone can buy this next month and get the same thing, they will wait. Every offer needs urgency that is real, not manufactured:

- Limited capacity ("We take 5 new clients per month")
- Time-bound pricing ("Price goes up after launch week")
- Seasonal relevance ("Get summer-ready in 8 weeks")
- Cohort-based ("Next intake starts March 1st")

If there's no reason to act now, your ads will get clicks but not conversions.

### 3. Is the risk reversed?

The prospect is thinking "what if it doesn't work?" Answer that before they ask:

- Money-back guarantee
- Free trial period
- "Pay when you see results"
- Case study showing someone just like them

The lower the perceived risk, the higher the conversion rate. If you have no risk reversal, your CPL will be 2-5x higher than it should be.

### 4. Is the price justified?

The prospect compares price to perceived value, not to your costs. Stack the value:

- What's included (sessions, access, materials, support)
- What it would cost them to NOT solve this (lost revenue, wasted time, continued pain)
- What alternatives cost (competitors, DIY, doing nothing)

If the value stack doesn't make the price feel like a deal, either add more value or lower the price. Don't try to fix it with better ads.

### 5. Is there proof it works?

Before running a single ad, you need at least one of:

- [ ] A customer testimonial with a specific result
- [ ] A case study with numbers
- [ ] Before/after evidence
- [ ] Third-party reviews (Google, Trustpilot)
- [ ] Your own results using the method

Zero proof = zero trust. See `reference/proof-hierarchy.md` for the full proof framework.

---

## Red Flags -- Don't Run Ads Yet If...

- You haven't sold the offer to anyone yet (validate with organic/outreach first)
- You can't explain the offer in one sentence
- There's no landing page or booking system ready
- You don't know your target CPL (what can you afford to pay per lead?)
- The offer has no differentiation from competitors (why you over them?)
