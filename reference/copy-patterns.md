# Ad Copy Patterns

Proven patterns for primary text, headlines, descriptions, and CTAs. Use these as templates -- fill in your specifics.

---

## Primary Text Patterns

Primary text is what appears above the creative in the feed. Keep it short. If someone has to click "See more", you have already lost most of them.

### Rules
- 5-6 lines maximum (before the fold)
- No emojis in ad copy -- they reduce trust in professional services
- Write at a 6th grade reading level -- the "6-year-old rule"
- One offer per ad -- never stack multiple CTAs
- First line is the hook -- it must earn the second line

### Short Copy (2-3 lines) -- Best for cold traffic
```
[Hook -- problem or result statement]
[One line of proof or mechanism]
[CTA with link]
```

### Medium Copy (4-6 lines) -- Best for warm traffic
```
[Hook -- bold claim or question]
[Agitate -- what it costs them to stay stuck]
[Solution -- what you do differently]
[Proof -- one specific result]
[CTA]
```

### Long Copy (7-10 lines) -- Best for retargeting or high-ticket
```
[Hook]
[Story -- 2-3 lines of context]
[Turning point]
[Result with specifics]
[Objection handler]
[CTA + urgency if genuine]
```

## Headline Patterns

Headlines appear below the creative, next to the CTA button. Keep them under 40 characters. They should reinforce, not repeat, the primary text.

### Result-Led
- `[Number] [Result] in [Timeframe]`
- `Get [Result] Without [Pain]`
- `From [Before] to [After]`

### Curiosity-Led
- `The [Niche] Secret Nobody Talks About`
- `Why [Common Approach] Doesn't Work`
- `What [Number] [People] Know That You Don't`

### Direct
- `Book Your Free [Call/Session/Audit]`
- `[Number] Spots Left This Month`
- `Start Getting [Result] Today`

### Social Proof
- `Join [Number]+ [People] Who [Result]`
- `Rated [Score] by [Number] [People]`
- `[Name] Got [Result] in [Time]`

## Description Patterns

Descriptions appear as smaller text below the headline. Often truncated on mobile. Use them to handle one objection or add one proof point.

- `No contracts. Cancel anytime.`
- `Free strategy call -- no obligation.`
- `[Number] businesses already using this.`
- `Results in [timeframe] or your money back.`
- `Limited to [number] new clients per month.`

## CTA Button Mapping

| Goal | CTA Type | When to Use |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Lead gen (form) | `SIGN_UP` | Landing page with opt-in form |
| Book a call | `BOOK_TRAVEL` or `LEARN_MORE` | Calendar booking page |
| Learn more | `LEARN_MORE` | Sales page, VSL, or info page |
| Send message | `SEND_MESSAGE` | Messenger/DM funnels |
| Shop / buy | `SHOP_NOW` | E-commerce, low-ticket offers |
| Download | `DOWNLOAD` | Lead magnets, PDFs, guides |
| Get quote | `GET_QUOTE` | Service businesses, custom pricing |
| Follow page | `LEARN_MORE` | Follower/awareness campaigns |

## Hook Patterns for Copy

The first line of primary text follows the same hook patterns as video hooks. These are the highest-converting opener structures:

### Question Hooks
- `Still [doing painful thing] every [timeframe]?`
- `What if you could [result] without [sacrifice]?`
- `Why are [audience] still [outdated approach]?`

### Statement Hooks
- `[Number] [audience] switched to [solution] last month.`
- `[Result] is not about [common belief]. It is about [truth].`
- `I stopped [common thing] and [unexpected result].`

### Challenge Hooks
- `Most [audience] will never [result]. Here is why.`
- `Your [competitor/alternative] does not want you to see this.`
- `[Common advice] is the worst thing you can do for [goal].`

### Proof Hooks
- `[Name] went from [before] to [after] in [time].`
- `We generated [number] [result] for [client type] last [timeframe].`
- `This one change added [number] to our [metric].`

## Copy Testing Rules

1. Test hooks first -- the first line determines everything
2. Test 3 hook variations per ad creative
3. Keep the body and CTA the same when testing hooks
4. Once you find a winning hook, test body copy variations
5. Never test more than one variable at a time
6. Let each variation run for 3-5 days before judging
7. Minimum 500 impressions before making a call
