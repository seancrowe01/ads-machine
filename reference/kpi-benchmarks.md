# Meta Ads KPI Benchmarks

> **Niche note:** These benchmarks are based on health, fitness, and online coaching niches. For other niches, use as directional framework -- do not apply numbers literally.

Use this file when analysing any ad account. Compare the numbers against the relevant benchmarks and make specific recommendations.

## Funnel Types

There are three distinct funnel types. Identify which one is running before applying any benchmarks:

1. **Follower Funnel** -- Ad drives profile views -> followers -> DMs -> call booked
2. **Conversion / VSL Funnel** -- Ad drives to landing page -> opt-in -> email/SMS follow-up -> call booked
3. **Messenger Funnel** -- Ad opens a DM conversation directly (benchmarks below)

Each funnel has its own KPIs. Do not mix them.

## Important -- Niche Scope

The specific numbers below come from health, fitness, and online coaching campaigns. They are the primary reference for those niches.

For accounts outside this niche (gyms, clinics, service businesses, etc.) do NOT apply these numbers directly. Instead:
- Use the benchmarks as a directional framework (same logic, different thresholds)
- Flag that industry-specific benchmarks are not on file and note what you are using as a reference point
- Apply the same decision logic (is CPL improving? is the funnel converting? is ROAS positive?) without holding the account to coaching-specific numbers
- If you have benchmarks for a different niche, add them to this file

## Funnel 1: Follower Ad Funnel

**How it works:** Ad -> Instagram profile view -> follow -> DM conversation -> call booked -> client signed

**Best for:** Coaches with budgets under $50/day. Lower friction than conversion funnel. Good starting point before scaling to VSL/conversion.

**Funnel flow benchmark:** 10-15% of profile views will follow. DM all qualified followers immediately.

### KPIs

| KPI | Target | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Cost per profile view | Under $0.50 | Primary ad efficiency metric |
| Cost per follower | Under $2 | Can still be profitable up to ~$5 |
| Cost per message sent | Under $5 | Only message qualified prospects |
| Cost per call booked (B2C) | $100-$150 | Health, fitness, wellness coaching |
| Cost per call booked (B2B) | $200-$400 | Business coaching, consulting |
| Cost per new client | ~$500 | 20-25% of program price |

**Most important metric:** Cost per call booked. Everything else is directional. If calls are cheap, the funnel is working regardless of the other numbers.

### Troubleshooting Decision Tree

**Profile views cost more than $0.50:**
- Fix the ad. Better hook, better creative, more attention-grabbing opening.

**Profile views are fine but followers cost more than $2:**
- Profile and content are not matching the ad. Improve the bio, post better content, make sure the ad reflects what is actually on the account.

**Getting followers but they are low quality (not worth messaging):**
- The ad is not calling out the ideal client specifically enough. Tighten the avatar callout in the ad copy.

**Getting messages started but not booking calls:**
- DM script or follow-up is the problem, not the ads. Follow up 2-3 times. Review the opener and the setting process. This is a sales skill issue, not an ads issue.

### Tracking -- Daily (Follower Funnel)

Track every day:
1. Amount spent
2. Profile views
3. New followers
4. Messages sent (qualified)
5. Calls booked
6. Calls taken
7. New clients signed
8. Contracted revenue
9. Collected revenue

## Funnel 3: Messenger Ad Funnel

**How it works:** Ad opens a DM conversation directly in Messenger/Instagram -> qualify in chat -> call booked -> client signed

**Best for:** Coaches starting out. Lowest barrier to entry. $30-50/day minimum. Fastest to get leads flowing (day 1-2).

### KPIs -- B2C (Health, Fitness, Wellness Coaching)

| KPI | Target | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Cost per conversation | Under $10 | Primary efficiency metric for this funnel |
| Cost per call booked | ~$150 | |
| Cost per new client | $500-$750 | 20-25% of program price |
| ROAS -- cash collected | 2-3x minimum | |
| ROAS -- contracted revenue | 5-7x target | |
| Minimum starting budget | $30/day | Aim for $50/day |
| Expected leads at $50/day | 3-5 new conversations/day | |

### KPIs -- B2B / Business Coaching

| KPI | Target | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Cost per conversation | Under $25 | |
| Cost per call booked | ~$250 | |
| Cost per new client | $1,000-$2,000 | 20-25% of program price |
| ROAS -- cash collected | 2-3x minimum | |
| ROAS -- contracted revenue | 5-7x target | |
| Minimum starting budget | $100/day | |
| Expected leads at $100/day | 3-5 new conversations/day | |

### Timeline Expectations (Messenger Funnel)

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| First conversations | Day 1-2 |
| First calls booked | End of week 1 or start of week 2 |
| First client signed | Day 14-21 |

**If conversations are coming in but no calls:** DM qualifying and setting process needs work. Follow up consistently, refine the opener.
**If no conversations by day 2:** Ad creative or targeting problem. Review hook and audience.

## Funnel 2: Conversion / VSL Funnel

**How it works:** Ad -> landing page -> opt-in -> email/SMS/DM follow-up -> call booked -> client signed

**Best for:** Coaches ready to scale. More friction, higher CPL, but more automated. Requires bigger budget and 14-21 days to optimise.

### KPIs -- B2C Coaching (Health, Fitness, Wellness, Life Coaching)

Applies when: offer is priced $1,000-$3,000 and targets consumers (not businesses).

| KPI | Target | Action if missed |
|-----|--------|-----------------|
| Cost per click (CPC) | Under $5 | Review ad creative -- hook or visual is likely weak |
| Cost per lead (CPL) | Under $15 | Check landing page conversion rate first, then creative |
| Cost per call booked | ~$150 | Check lead follow-up systems (email, SMS, DMs) |
| Cost per new client | $500-$1,000 | Check close rate on calls -- ads may be fine, sales is the issue |
| ROAS -- cash collected | 2-3x minimum | Below this = not sustainable, investigate full funnel |
| ROAS -- contracted revenue | 5-7x target | This is the ceiling goal, not day 1 expectation |
| Minimum starting budget | $100/day | Below this = not enough data to optimise |
| Optimal starting budget | $150/day | Ensures roughly 1 call booked per day |

**Funnel conversion benchmarks (B2C):**
- Lead to call booked: ~10% (1 in 10 leads should book a call)
- Call to client: ~25% (1 in 4 calls should close)
- Lead to client: ~2.5% overall

**Offer pricing rule:**
- Cost per client should be 20-25% of program price
- Minimum viable offer price: $2,000
- Example: $2,500 program = can spend up to $500-$625 to acquire a client profitably

---

### KPIs -- B2B / Business Coaching / Small Niche

Applies when: offer is business coaching, consulting, marketing, sales training, or any niche with a small total addressable market.

| KPI | Target | Action if missed |
|-----|--------|-----------------|
| Cost per click (CPC) | Under $10 | Audience too broad or creative not relevant enough |
| Cost per lead (CPL) | Under $25 | Review landing page and offer relevance |
| Cost per call booked | ~$250 | Strengthen follow-up sequences |
| Cost per new client | $1,000-$2,000 | Review close rate and offer positioning |
| ROAS -- cash collected | 2-3x minimum | |
| ROAS -- contracted revenue | 5-7x target | |
| Minimum starting budget | $250/day | Smaller TAM requires more spend to find buyers |

**Offer pricing rule:**
- Offer must be $5,000-$10,000 minimum for B2B ads to be profitable
- Cost per client = 20-25% of program price
- Example: $5,000 offer = can spend $1,000-$1,250 to acquire profitably
- At $1,000 offer price, max acquisition spend = $200-$250 -- very tight, not recommended

## Timeline Expectations

Use when evaluating whether to keep a campaign running:

| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| First leads | Day 1 (within hours of launch) |
| First calls booked | Day 7-14 (requires follow-up systems in place) |
| First client signed | Day 14-21 (sometimes end of month 1) |
| Campaign optimised | 30-60 days of consistent spend |

**If no leads by end of day 1:** Creative or targeting problem. Check ad delivery and CPM first.
**If leads are coming in but no calls:** Follow-up system is broken. Check email/SMS sequences.
**If calls are coming in but no clients:** Sales problem, not an ads problem. Do not kill the campaign.

## Tracking System -- What to Monitor Weekly

Every ad account should track these numbers per week:

1. Amount spent
2. Impressions (people who saw the ads)
3. Clicks
4. Leads (opt-ins/applications)
5. Calls booked
6. Calls taken
7. New clients signed
8. Revenue generated (cash collected + contracted)

---

## Decision Rules -- When to Scale vs Kill

**Scale an ad if:**
- CPL is under benchmark for 7+ days
- Lead to call rate is at or above 10%
- ROAS cash collected is 2x or above

**Kill an ad if:**
- Spent 2x the target CPL with zero leads
- CPL is 3x the benchmark after 500+ impressions with no improvement
- CPC is consistently over 2x benchmark

**Never kill for:**
- Slow start (first 48-72 hours -- algorithm is learning)
- High CPL in week 1 before optimisation kicks in
- Low volume on a new audience (give it spend)

## Budget Minimums

| Rule | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Minimum monthly ad spend (done-for-you) | $1,000/month |
| Daily equivalent | ~$35/day |
| Target lead volume at this spend | 1 lead per day |
| Required CPL at minimum budget | Under $35 |
| Budget planning window | 28 days (never burst spend) |

**Risk framing:** Only spend what the business can afford to lose entirely. State this clearly before starting.

**Back-end marketing multiplier:** Email sequences, upsells, and cross-sells post-purchase dramatically improve effective ROAS without additional ad spend. Always factor this into profitability conversations.

## Creative Performance Metrics

Two key metrics visible in Ads Manager that measure creative quality before CPL data is meaningful:

| Metric | What It Measures | Strong Signal |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Hook Rate** | % of viewers who watch past the first 3 seconds | Measures if the opening hook works |
| **Hold Rate** | % who continue watching beyond the hook | Measures sustained engagement |

**Interpretation:** Strong hook rate + strong hold rate = reliable early signal of creative performance. Use these to kill underperforming creatives before spending heavily.

**Attention spans (for context):**
- Gen Z: ~8 seconds
- Millennials: ~12 seconds
- Baby Boomers: ~20 seconds

## CTR Benchmark

**1%+ CTR (link clicks) = front-end is working.**

- Above 1% = creative and audience are doing their job. Problem is downstream (landing page, funnel, offer).
- Below 1% = creative or audience problem. Fix the hook or try broader targeting.

At launch: start with 3 ad creatives, 3 headlines, 3 body copy variants. Monitor CTR first before assessing anything else.

---

## Frequency Threshold

**TOF / cold audience frequency cap: 2.5**

- Frequency above 2.5 on cold audiences = ad fatigue signal. Pause and replace the campaign.
- Seeing the same ad 3 or more times by cold audiences = diminishing returns.
- Monitor frequency at the ad set level. Readings of 2.44-2.95 are the trigger zone.

## Review Cadence

| Monthly Spend | Review Frequency |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Under $10,000/month | Once per week |
| $10,000-$15,000+/month | Twice per week |

**Per review:** Check campaign, ad set, and ad level against benchmarks. Flag for kill/watch/scale. Log findings.

**Test duration before drawing conclusions:**
- Standard accounts: 2-3 days minimum per change
- Low-spend accounts (under $35/day): 3-5 days before concluding

---

## Cash Flow Warning

The business must be cash positive in month 1. Flag this if offer pricing is misaligned:

- Charging $300-$500/month recurring + acquisition cost $500 = losing money
- Push paid in full or 3-6 month packages on the front end, then roll to monthly
- Minimum viable program price for B2C: $2,000
- Minimum viable program price for B2B: $5,000

---

## Funnel Benchmarks (Conversion Funnel)

**Landing page opt-in rate:** 20-25% of visitors should give their name, email, and phone number.
- Below 20% = landing page problem, not an ads problem. Fix the headline or offer before scaling spend.

**Application page warning:** Adding an application step before the booking page cuts call volume by ~50%. Do not recommend this unless there are more calls than can be handled.

**Lead-to-buyer timeline:** On average 40-45 days from opt-in to purchase. Do not judge ROAS in the first 30 days. Set this expectation upfront.

**B2B exception:** B2B accounts can skip the opt-in page and drive traffic directly to a video or offer page. They still need retargeting set up for people who land and leave.

---

## When Analysing an Ad Account

Always do this in order:
1. Pull last 7 and 30 day data
2. Identify which funnel type is running (Follower / Messenger / Conversion)
3. Compare each KPI against the relevant benchmark (B2C or B2B)
4. Identify which part of the funnel is broken (clicks, leads, calls, close)
5. Give one clear recommendation per problem, not a list of 10 things
6. Flag any cash flow risk if ROAS is below 2x cash collected
7. Never judge ROAS before day 40-45 on a conversion funnel
