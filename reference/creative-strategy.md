# Meta Ads Creative Strategy

Use this file when briefing, producing, scripting, or reviewing ad creatives. Covers creative principles, formats, scripting, copy rules, production workflow, and testing.

---

## The Creative Hierarchy

**Most important to least important:**

1. **Messaging** -- script, pain/solution fit, audience relevance
2. **Hook / First frame** -- visually engaging before audio starts
3. **Retention** -- keep people watching past the hook
4. **CTA** -- tell people what to do

Creative does 50%+ of the selling on Meta. Copy is supplementary. Ads Manager headline appears below the creative on Instagram feed -- the creative does the heavy lifting.

---

## The Ad Does the Targeting

Detailed audience targeting no longer works the way it used to. The words in the ad and body copy put the ad in front of the right people.

**Speak directly to your ICP in the creative.** If you want to reach 35-year-old female fitness coaches, say that in the ad -- do not try to engineer it through audience settings.

---

## Creative Performance Metrics

Track these in Ads Manager to assess creative quality early, before CPL data is available:

| Metric | Definition | What It Tells You |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Hook Rate** | % of viewers who watch past the first 3 seconds | Whether the opening hook works |
| **Hold Rate** | % who continue watching beyond the hook | Whether sustained engagement holds |

Both strong = reliable early signal of a winning creative. Weak hook rate = fix the opener. Good hook rate but weak hold rate = the body is losing people.

**Attention spans (for context):**
- Gen Z: ~8 seconds average
- Millennials: ~12 seconds average
- Baby Boomers: ~20 seconds average

**First 3 seconds are critical.** The first frame (thumbnail before autoplay) is equally important -- it must stop the scroll before audio begins.

## Ad Script Formula

**Hook -> Pain Points -> Outcomes/Benefits -> Story/Credibility -> CTA**

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Hook** | Opening line -- stops the scroll, grabs attention immediately |
| **Pain Points** | Speak directly to your ideal customer's suffering; creates relevance |
| **Outcomes/Benefits** | Ideal future state after using product/service; lead with outcomes not features |
| **Story / Credibility** | Social proof, track record -- "Over the past X years, I've helped X people achieve..." |
| **CTA** | Clear next action |

**Structured script template:**
1. **Identification** -- "Are you someone who [problem/pain point]?"
2. **Empathy** -- "I understand..." (you are not alone)
3. **Story/Credibility** -- "Over the past X years, I've helped X people achieve [Outcome 1, 2, 3]"
4. **CTA** -- Book a call / go to site / DM us

**Social proof stat format:** "Over the past [X] years, I've helped over [X] [audience type] achieve [outcome 1], [outcome 2], [outcome 3]"

## Inclusion and Exclusion Messaging

Every creative should make clear: **who this is FOR and who it is NOT FOR.**

Being explicit about who it's not for actually increases conversion quality by self-selecting the audience. Don't be vague -- commit to both sides.

Example: "This is NOT for business owners who are happy coasting. This IS for business owners who want predictable, scalable growth."

## Ad Format Library

Use a mix of formats across your ad set. Creative diversity = different concepts, angles, and formats -- not just the same video with different hooks.

| Format | When to Use | Production Notes |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| **Face-to-camera / Talking Head** | Trust, authority, expert positioning | B-roll overlays used to visualise what's being said |
| **UGC (User Generated Content)** | Authentic cold audience appeal; essential for DTC/e-com | Authentic feel, multiple scene changes, captions |
| **Customer Testimonial** | Social proof, post-purchase trust | Real names, real photos, real context |
| **Case Study Walkthrough** | Results-driven audiences | How-to format showing the process and result |
| **Interview Style** | On-the-spot Q&A, raw opinions | Works because it feels off-the-cuff, unedited |
| **Repurposed Podcast Clips** | Third-party delivery reduces perceived selling | Clip to the segment relevant to your offer; must hit pain point and outcome |
| **Concept / Role Play / Skits** | Pattern interrupt, brand awareness | High recall; props and costumes don't need to be expensive |

**UGC Structure (fundamental formula):**
1. Hook (attention grab)
2. Retention hack (keep them watching -- scene change, B-roll, movement)
3. Call to action
4. Supporting elements: subtitles, headline overlay, B-roll overlays

**Third-party delivery** (customer testimonial, podcast guest) reduces perceived selling pressure -- more persuasive than a direct pitch from the business owner.

## Content Pillars to Test

Always test multiple pillars -- don't default to the same format repeatedly. When you get a strong result from one type, double down.

- Client journey / customer experience walkthrough
- Testimonials and case study walkthroughs
- Pain-point led / direct response
- Humour-based (high shareability, brand recall -- best for cold TOF)
- Founder/team story (personal brand, face behind the business)
- Authority signals (awards, credentials, press coverage, podcast appearances)
- Behind-the-scenes / day in the life
- ASMR (on-trend for local/service businesses -- amplify ambient sound)
- Contrast structure ("Here's what you're used to -> here's why we're different")

## B-Roll Shot List

B-roll clips are 3-5 seconds each. Build a B-roll library: capture once, reuse across multiple campaigns.

**Core B-Roll Categories:**

| Category | Shot Examples |
|----------|--------------|
| Zone of Genius / Working | Video calls, typing proposals, reviewing campaigns, sketching on whiteboards |
| Leading + Teaching | Presenting to team, workshop delivery, coaching 1-to-1 |
| Behind the Brand | Setting up workspace, laptop open, coffee shop/office |
| Client Success + Results | Scrolling testimonials, Google reviews screenshots, reacting to happy client messages, before/after visuals |
| Authority Signals | Being interviewed, podcast recording, Q&A delivery |
| Planning + Strategy | Post-it notes, mind maps, whiteboard sessions, voice notes |

**"Hyper-Reality" technique:** You can simulate meetings and scenarios -- get 3-4 friends/family, sit them at a table or on Zoom, record from behind. It doesn't have to be real.

**Show + Tell beats just Tell.** Anyone can make claims. Showing them visually builds trust faster and creates a clearer path to conversion.

## Production Rules

**Shoot format:**
- Always shoot in **9:16 vertical format** for mobile-first
- Main subject positioned in the **middle third** of the frame
- Captions/subtitles sit in the **middle third** -- not near edges where autocrop cuts off
- One vertical video auto-crops to multiple placements if composed correctly

**High production vs batch shoots:**
- Full-day hero shoot (with lighting, multiple locations, wardrobe) = **3-4 finished ads**
- Actual shooting time on a "full day" = ~4 hours (subtract travel, setup, lunch, mistakes)
- High-production shoots are for evergreen hero ads -- not for generating volume
- Batch/low-production UGC shoots are better for volume and testing

**Key tactic -- record full script in every location:**
- Don't record just individual lines per location
- Record the entire script in each environment
- 3 locations x 1 full script each = multiple distinct-feeling ads from one shoot

**Production quality:** Shoot on iPhone. Lower production = more authentic = less friction for cold audiences. UGC consistently outperforms polished TV-style ads on Meta.

## Creative Testing

**Start with 3 hook variants per core video** -- the body stays the same, only the first 3 seconds changes. Monitor hook rate in Ads Manager to find the best opener.

**Split test budget rule:** Don't split a small budget across too many creatives. Spreading budget too thin = no statistically valid data.

| Daily Budget | Max simultaneous creative tests |
|-------------|--------------------------------|
| $35/day | 3 creatives |
| $10/day | 3 creatives (at the limit) |

**Test window:** 3 days minimum per change. 3-5 days for low-spend accounts. Don't leave tests open-ended.

**Act on results:**
- Clear winner = push more budget to it, retire the loser
- Replace losers with new challengers immediately -- always have a challenger running against your control

**Never run a single creative in isolation.** Always at least 2 variants.

## Ad Copy Rules

**Short-form is the standard.** Aim for 5-6 lines maximum in the primary text field. Long-form is largely ineffective on Meta now.

**Copywriting principles:**
1. **Use questions** -- self-qualify the audience and increase hold rate ("Are you a business owner struggling with lead flow?")
2. **Simplify the offer** -- the video creative does more than half the selling; copy reduces friction
3. **The 6-year-old rule** -- if a 6-year-old couldn't understand it, it's too complex
4. **Make a big promise** -- state a clear outcome or transformation directly
5. **Speak to existing belief systems** -- you're finding people already convinced they need the solution, not converting sceptics
6. **Specific callout in the first line** -- stronger self-qualification filter
7. **No emojis** -- can distract from the message, reduce conversions, and clash with brand tone

**Generate headlines separately from body copy.**
Use 5 headline variations for split testing.
AI tools get you ~80% of the way -- edit the remaining 20%.
Always strip AI-generated section headers before pasting into Ads Manager.

## CapCut Workflow

Primary editing tool for ad creatives. Faster iteration than Adobe Premiere Pro.

**Key features for ad production:**

| Feature | Use Case |
|---------|---------|
| Split tool (A/B key) | Cut clips, remove dead sections, isolate best takes |
| Auto Captions | Non-negotiable for feed ads -- most watched without sound |
| Remove Background | React-style content over news articles, reports, competitor content |
| Masks / Split Screen | Picture-in-picture, side-by-side comparisons |
| Ratio / Crop | Format for different placements (9:16, 1:1, 16:9) |
| Overlays / Layering | B-roll on top of talking head -- highest track = most visible |
| Filters / Colour Grading | Enhance quality to match brand tone |
| Music + Audio | Add music synced to cuts -- drives retention |

**Workflow rules:**
- Set the correct ratio before editing -- don't edit in the wrong format and crop after
- Record in central frame -> easy crop to any aspect ratio in post
- Music synced to clip cuts drives retention in video ads

## Creative Audit Checklist

Before publishing any ad, check:

- [ ] First frame stops the scroll before audio
- [ ] Hook lands in first 3 seconds
- [ ] Inclusion/exclusion clear (who it's for AND who it's not for)
- [ ] Script follows Hook -> Pain -> Benefit -> Story -> CTA
- [ ] Captions/subtitles on (most watched without sound)
- [ ] Correct aspect ratio for placement (9:16 for reels/stories)
- [ ] Subject in middle third of frame
- [ ] B-roll overlays support spoken claims (show + tell)
- [ ] Body copy 5-6 lines max, no emojis
- [ ] CTA is explicit and clear
- [ ] Naming convention applied consistently
