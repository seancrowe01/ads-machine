# Visual Styles and Production Specs

Reference guide for ad creative formats, production requirements, and visual best practices.

---

## Aspect Ratios

| Ratio | Dimensions | Where It Runs |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 9:16 | 1080x1920 | Stories, Reels, TikTok (primary for video) |
| 4:5 | 1080x1350 | Feed posts (primary for feed placement) |
| 1:1 | 1080x1080 | Feed posts, carousel cards |
| 16:9 | 1920x1080 | YouTube pre-roll, in-stream |

**Default recommendation:** Shoot in 9:16 (vertical). Crop to 4:5 for feed if needed. Most impressions come from mobile feed and stories.

## Video Ad Formats

### UGC Talking Head
- Single person speaking directly to camera
- Phone or webcam quality is fine -- polished looks like an ad, raw looks like content
- Subject in middle third of frame
- Good lighting on face (natural light or ring light)
- Duration: 15-30 seconds for cold traffic, 30-60 for warm
- Best for: testimonials, founder stories, direct pitches

### UGC Testimonial
- Customer sharing their experience on camera
- Must feel unscripted even if guided
- Include specific numbers or results
- Show the person, not just a voiceover
- Duration: 30-60 seconds
- Best for: social proof, warm retargeting

### Screen Recording / Demo
- Screen capture showing a tool, process, or result
- Voiceover or text overlay explaining what is happening
- Zoom in on key moments -- do not show full desktop
- Add cursor highlights or annotations
- Duration: 15-45 seconds
- Best for: SaaS, tools, before/after, tutorials

### Split Screen
- Top half: talking head or text
- Bottom half: screen recording or B-roll
- Keeps attention by giving two things to look at
- Duration: 15-30 seconds
- Best for: demos with commentary, reaction-style content

### Motion Graphics
- Animated text, icons, or illustrations
- No filming required -- built in tools like Remotion, After Effects, or Canva
- Strong for communicating data, stats, or step-by-step processes
- Duration: 15-30 seconds
- Best for: awareness campaigns, educational content, brand building

### Static Image
- Single image with text overlay
- Must communicate the message without needing to read the caption
- Headline on image should be 5-7 words maximum
- High contrast between text and background
- Best for: simple offers, event promotions, retargeting

### Carousel
- 3-10 cards, each with an image or graphic
- Each card should make sense on its own AND as part of the sequence
- First card is the hook -- it must earn the swipe
- Last card is the CTA
- Best for: multi-step processes, multiple testimonials, product features

## B-Roll Categories

Stock these for use in video ads. 8 core categories:

1. **Working shots** -- typing, phone scrolling, writing, laptop screen
2. **Lifestyle shots** -- coffee, walking, gym, morning routine
3. **Results shots** -- dashboard, notifications, money, metrics
4. **Pain shots** -- frustration, overwhelm, clock ticking, empty calendar
5. **Transformation shots** -- before/after spaces, messy to clean
6. **Social proof shots** -- testimonial screenshots, review screenshots, DMs
7. **Product/tool shots** -- your tool in action, close-ups of interface
8. **Location shots** -- office, gym, studio, wherever your audience works

## Text Overlay Rules

- Maximum 5 words per line on screen
- Maximum 2 lines at once
- High contrast: white text on dark background or black text on light
- Add a semi-transparent background box behind text if the video is busy
- Text should appear for minimum 2 seconds
- Sync text changes to speech or music beats
- Hook text (first 3 seconds) should be larger than body text
- Never cover the speaker's face with text

## Text Positions
- **Top third:** Hook text, attention-grabbing statements
- **Center:** Main message, key points
- **Bottom third:** CTAs, captions, subtitles (avoid -- often covered by platform UI)

## Production Checklist

### Before Filming
- [ ] Script or filming card printed/visible
- [ ] Camera at eye level (not looking up or down)
- [ ] Subject in middle third of 9:16 frame
- [ ] Lighting on face (window light, ring light, or soft box)
- [ ] Clean background (not distracting)
- [ ] Audio check -- quiet room, no echo
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb

### During Filming
- [ ] Look at camera lens, not screen
- [ ] Speak at conversational pace -- not too fast
- [ ] Pause between sections (easier to edit)
- [ ] Film the hook 3 times with different energy
- [ ] Film B-roll before or after main take

### After Filming
- [ ] Review audio quality on headphones
- [ ] Check framing on mobile (not just desktop)
- [ ] Export in highest quality available
- [ ] File naming: `[ClientCode]_[Angle]_[Format]_[Date]_raw`

## Thumbnail / First Frame

The first frame of a video ad acts as a thumbnail in some placements. Make it count:

- Should communicate the topic without playing the video
- Include text overlay with the hook (3-5 words)
- Show a face if it is a talking head ad
- High contrast, bright colors
- Test 3 thumbnail variations per video
